Pair Programming - An awesome experience

Jun 21



Over the last couple months, I have been pair programming a lot, at least 3 times a week with fellow boots. Its been an amazing experience and I am loving every moment doing it. I am enjoying it so much that I tend to look for a partner/pair to work with when something cool idea clicks on my mind.

While I am on Phase 0 at Dev Bootcamp, part of the assignments are supposed to be done with pair. Besides those assignments, I have had many moments, many programming challenges, hacks and tricks I wanted to work on, and on each and every one of those, I have been inviting other people to pair program with me.

Here are few examples:-

  1. Pairing is Caring with Matt Darin
  2. VolunteerIsrael with Arik Gadye
  3. BerkeleyX: CS169.1x with Arik, Marek, Leyla and Patrick
  4. DomainsHack : Looking for partner to pair with for a weekend, possibly after DBC.
  5. tryRefills with Jesse Wolgamott and Jesse Waites

While Pair Programming, I have been learning a lot of tricks, hacks and things that I would have never figured out by myself. It could either be some cool Productivity tools and shortcuts (Sublime Text Shortcuts, Alfred + Dash Integration, terminal themes and tricks or some pure ruby/js/html/rails).


Learning goes both ways, I have also been able to teach what I know while pairing with a partner and it feels good to teach, I seriously love the experience of two way learning. Pair programming in addition to a learning experience, can also be taken as a code reviews session. One person drives while other navigates. While driving, we could make a lot of mistakes, spelling or basic stupid syntax error, while are often time consuming when you are on your own and you have a failing code just because of stupid semi-colon or something of that nature.


With all that being said, I am always open to pair programming sessions. If you have cool idea that you would like to pair on, or a problem you are stuck with for hours/days, count me in, lets schedule a pair programming session and solve it, lets do it...
